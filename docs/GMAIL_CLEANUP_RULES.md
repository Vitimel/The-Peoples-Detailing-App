# Gmail Cleanup Rules

Last updated: 2026-05-26

## Goal

Keep the inbox for current, actionable, or emotionally important mail. Keep saved labels lean. Trash stale clutter and stale records, but do not permanently delete.

## Always Protect Or Save

- Starred mail, Gmail-important mail, `Keep - Important`, and `Review Needed`.
- Security, login, verification, password reset, MFA, account access, device-linking, and privacy-setting notices.
- Legal, tax, IRS, H&R Block, healthcare, school, family, property, lease, court, and account notices.
- Product setup, warranty, return, support/RMA, software access, device setup, and meaningful attachment mail.
- Current Rocket Money budget/spending emails.
- Legal, tax, IRS, court, and truly active account notices.

## Archive And Label

- Save records under the closest label: `Saved/Finance`, `Saved/Receipts`, `Saved/Product Setup`, `Saved/Family-Melody`, `Saved/School`, or `Saved/Tax-Legal`.
- Apply `Cleanup/Archived` before removing `INBOX`.
- Apply `Cleanup/Protected` to sensitive account, legal, healthcare, school, security, or family messages.
- Recent finance/mailbox history should currently mean about the last week unless it is Rocket Money, legal, tax, account access, school, or family-related.
- Old bank, payment app, utility, loan, credit, PayPal, Venmo, Xfinity, OneMain, Snap, Public, and similar finance records are usually not needed after the newest week; move stale copies to Trash unless they are current action items or tax/legal records.
- Credit-monitoring alerts from Credit Karma/LendingTree should keep only the newest couple weeks in the inbox; older alerts can stay labeled `Keep - Important` but should be archived to `Saved/Finance`/`Cleanup/Protected`.
- Product, device, parental-control, account setup, and service access messages should be saved to `Saved/Product Setup` or `Cleanup/Protected`, then archived.

## Trash

- Old job leads and hiring blasts when not actively job hunting.
- Old retail, grocery, food delivery, restaurant, game, betting, entertainment, and newsletter marketing.
- Old Walmart/grocery/food receipts unless they show a refund, return, dispute, warranty, unusual charge, or active issue.
- Old apartment/townhouse/property lead emails unless they are an active lease, fee, notice, or current property manager message.
- Old ClassDojo or school chatter beyond about one month unless it has pictures, current admin action, forms, payments, or meaningful active kid/school context.
- Stale seller-rating prompts, surveys, social notifications, and expired promo/deal emails.
- Old Google/Gmail/Google Photos storage warnings and photo-print nudges after they are no longer current.
- Generic school flyers and calendar reminders unless they mention pictures, payments, forms, admin action, or meaningful family context.
- Mortgage, prequalification, refinance, apartment, townhouse, property, and rent lead/marketing emails unless currently shopping or tied to a real lease, fee, notice, or manager.
- DoorDash DashPass payment-failed notices and Audible payment-update-required notices can be trashed for now unless the user later says those subscriptions matter again.
- Completed Obsidian account activation / Sync setup emails can be trashed after setup is confirmed.
- Stale login codes and security notifications for Google, OpenAI/ChatGPT, Shopify, Microsoft, and similar services can be trashed once they are old enough that the code no longer works or the user confirms the event is handled.
- U.S. Bank e-statement notices can be trashed because statements are available inside the account.
- Old illegal-dumping/property-manager notice threads can be trashed when the user explicitly calls them unneeded.
- Costco receipts can be trashed unless tied to returns, warranty, tax, or a current dispute.
- Saved receipts older than about one week can be trashed unless they are tax/legal, warranty/return/dispute, or active-account records.
- Saved finance older than about one week can be trashed unless it is Rocket Money, tax/legal, or a current action item.
- Anthropic/Claude emails can be trashed.
- Google Play emails can be trashed.
- Affirm emails can be trashed.
- Old ConnectNetwork, GettingOut, GTL, CorrectPay, commissary, and similar no-reply receipts can be trashed unless they are from about the last week or tied to a current dispute/action.
- Old product/setup/verification emails can be trashed once setup is complete, including stale Zazzle, VIZIO, Glide, Lovable, Pixel Watch, Amazon Echo, AT&T order-review, and generic support-feedback messages.
- Old school newsletters with bulky inline images can be trashed unless they mention a current action, payment, pictures, forms, or meaningful family/school context.
- Delivery-failure notices and typo-address duplicates can be trashed after confirming the important message was resent correctly or is no longer needed.
- Old ClassDojo receipts/invoices can be trashed unless from about the last week or tied to a current dispute/action.

## Current Known Direct Rules

- Trash old sender families like job lead domains once sampled and confirmed.
- Trash old Uber Eats, Walmart grocery, restaurant, retail, FIFA, Mistplay, Carter's, PacSun, GNC, and similar promo/receipt clutter unless a protected keyword or useful record is present.
- Archive Sequium/T-Mobile account notices only when current/actionable; otherwise stale copies can be trashed.
- Archive PlayStation, Xfinity, Public, Venmo, bank, and loan records only when recent/current. Google Play and Affirm can be trashed. Audible purchase/account history can be trashed unless currently needed; current payment-update nags can be trashed for now.
- Archive Amazon/Alexa/VIZIO account, parental, device-linking, and setup messages as protected/product setup.
- Leave starred messages alone unless explicitly reviewed.
- Keep W2/check PDFs, tax returns, Melody/TennCare/POA documents, starred work documents, and legal/healthcare/government PDFs unless the user explicitly says they are no longer needed.

## Safety Checks

- Before trashing a new sender family, read a sample first.
- Do not trash anything with clear protected labels or sensitive subjects without local inspection.
- After major trash batches, check recent Trash for security, tax, school, healthcare, finance, login, and password-related matches.
- Prefer moving exact message IDs over broad bulk mutation when there is any chance of mixed records.
